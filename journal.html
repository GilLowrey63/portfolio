Creation Journal

In this journal I am keeping track of my journey to learn to code and create this site. My goal here is to show how I approach and work through problems.


6/20/20

I am starting this project June 20th, 2020. I have been coding for about a year now and I love it. I teach first grade now and in the beginning of 2019 I build my own arcade using a raspberry pi. I was showing my students one Friday before they went home, and one of them said I should make my own video game so I can put my owngame on the arcade I built. After I suggested to his parents he immediately be moved to the 10th grade for having such amazing ideas, I went home and looked up "how to learn to code". I found a few different sites that caught my interest, but I really liked codecademy.com. I was hooked. All summer I set aside "work time" so I could develop my skills. I worked through HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and ReactJS for the front end and I completed a few backend projects.

The librarian at our school approached me one day with some coding questions. She also let me know she was expected to learn how to code and to be able to teach the students. Knowing she was uncomfortable with this expectation and having a passion for coding, I asked if I could help in any way. We developed a plan our principal approved for me to continue teaching my first grade class with the exception of writing. I would teach coding during that time and our librarian would teach my class writing. I was ecstatic! In order to be certified though I would have to take classes. I enrolled at Sheridan County Community College (the only college program our state teaching board approved of at the time) and started with a class based in HTML/CSS and another based in JavaScript. I successfully completed both and decided Front End (with an interest in learning more Back End work) Development was for me.

I am now putting together a portfolio I hope to leverage into a job. These are the beginning steps of that goal and I look forward to learning more as I work on this project. I read somewhere that the goal should be to make a site where you already know 80% of what is required and learn the other 20%. I am aiming for a 70/30 split. I am hard coding the first few journal entries, but I plan on not needing to hard code all of them. I also want to leave areas for comments if anyone should stumble onto my page. I will start by utilizing my HTML/CSS/JavaScript skills, and I will create a page or two to show my ReactJS skills as well. I feel strong with HTML/CSS/JavaScript but my ReactJS skills need some development. I will start with a strong foot forward and go from there. I hope you have enjoyed reading.
